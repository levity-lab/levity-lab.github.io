Driving Laboratory Automation with Acoustic Levitation

At Levity, our mission is to lead innovation in robotic liquid handling and laboratory automation using acoustic levitation technology. We aim to provide automated, non-contact solutions that improve accuracy, speed, and safety in liquid management, reducing human error and contamination risks in sensitive environments. By applying our software controlled non-contact manipulation and sensing methods to laboratories, we can offer new automated liquid handling solutions for university research, diagnostics, and biotechnology industries.

Handling small volumes of liquids, particularly in pharmaceutical, diagnostic, and research environments, presents significant challenges:

  • Contamination Risks: Traditional pipetting and handling methods can lead to cross-contamination.
  • Precision Issues: Liquid handling methods can suffer from inconsistencies, impacting the accuracy of experimental results.
  • Efficiency Concerns: Manual handling of liquids is time-consuming and prone to human error, delaying research and production timelines.
  • Scalability Challenges: For high-throughput environments, scaling manual processes can be inefficient and error-prone.
Acoustic levitation offers a breakthrough solution for these challenges by enabling precise, non-contact liquid manipulation. With this technology, liquids can be moved, mixed, and dispensed with unparalleled accuracy and without the risk of contamination.

Our levitation system uses high-frequency sound waves to create a pressure field that can lift and control small volumes of liquids in mid-air. This technology offers precise handling capabilities without the need for direct contact with the liquid or its container. By leveraging advanced sound wave manipulation, the lab will provide:

  • Non-Contact Liquid Dispensing: The ability to dispense and move liquids without contamination risks or physical contact.
  • Precise Volume Control: High accuracy in handling even microliters of liquid for experimental and production purposes.
  • Automated Liquid Handling Systems: A fully automated system that can perform tasks like mixing, transferring, and dispensing liquids in a controlled environment, ideal for pharmaceutical applications and high-throughput settings.
  • High Throughput and Scalability: Automated systems can be scaled to handle large volumes of liquid in laboratory and production workflows.
